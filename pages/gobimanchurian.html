Gobi manchurian

Description:Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made with cauliflower florets that are coated in a batter and deep-fried, and then tossed in a spicy sauce. It is a delicious and versatile dish that can be served as an appetizer, snack, or main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup tomato ketchup
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Add the cauliflower florets to the bowl and toss to coat.
  3. Heat the oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  4. Add the cauliflower florets to the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per batch.
  5. Remove the cauliflower florets from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the tomato ketchup, soy sauce, vinegar, cornstarch, and water.
  7. Heat the sauce in a small saucepan over medium heat until thickened, about 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add the fried cauliflower florets to the sauce and toss to coat.
  9. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.
