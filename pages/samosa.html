Samosa

Description:

Samosa is a popular savory pastry from the Indian subcontinent. It is made with a flaky pastry dough that is filled with a savory filling of potatoes, peas, and spices. Samosas are typically deep-fried, but they can also be baked.

Ingredients:

    For the pastry:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 6 tablespoons water
  • For the filling:
  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup frozen green peas
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro leaves

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, carom seeds, and salt.
  2. Add the oil and rub it into the flour with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Add the water and mix until you form a dough.
  4. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, or until it is smooth and elastic.
  5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, make the filling. Heat the oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and mustard seeds and cook until they start to pop.
  7. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a minute, stirring constantly.
  8. Add the potatoes and peas and cook until heated through.
  9. Stir in the cilantro leaves and set aside.
  10. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces.
  11. Roll out each piece of dough into a 6-inch circle.
  12. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each circle.
  13. Fold the circle in half and seal the edges.
  14. Deep-fry the samosas in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.
  15. Serve hot with your favorite chutney.
